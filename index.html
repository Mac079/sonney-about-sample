SONNEY Paris, Our story

It began with
a garden
hose.

Not a mood board. Not a trend report. Not a fashion week. A garden hose in the Jura, where Lora Sonney grew up, that caught her eye and made her ask: what if this became something else entirely?

The origin

Material first.
Everything follows.

Lora Sonney grew up in Pontarlier, in the Jura region of France. A place of dense woodlands, cold air, and gardens that live at the edge of wildness. She absorbed it the way children absorb everything before they understand that they're absorbing it. The tools. The textures. The particular quality of light that makes moss look like velvet.

One day, a garden hose caught her attention. Not as a garden hose. As a material waiting to become something it had never been before. She spent months developing a softening process to transform rubber into something that moved like leather. Out of that process came bags with impossible textures. Out of those bags came a technique for fusing material into trompe-l'oeil prints that looked like leopard, like snakeskin, like things that don't exist in nature but feel like they should. The prints that are now SONNEY's signature came from a garden hose in the Jura. That is the whole story, in miniature.

"I love creating materials. It's my way of working. Then I build the collection around these experiments."

Lora Sonney, Founder
The philosophy

Nature as material.
Material as language.

SONNEY does not begin with a silhouette. It begins with a question about what a material can do. Every collection is built from the inside out, from the fabric, from the surface, from the texture first. The garment is the conclusion of an experiment, not the starting point.

This is why SONNEY's prints look like nothing else. They are not licensed or sourced. They are made, through a process of material research that began in a garden and continues in the studio. Every trompe-l'oeil, every surface illusion, every pattern that makes you look twice carries that origin inside it.

Formation

The training
behind the eye.

Lora studied at the Haute Ecole d'Art et de Design in Geneva. After graduating in 2021, she worked at Marine Serre and Celine before joining the Swiss embroidery house Jakob Schlaepfer, the atelier that supplies precious textiles to some of the most demanding fashion houses in the world. She learned what rigour looks like at scale. She kept the curiosity that makes it personal.

The making

Made in Europe.
Made deliberately.

Every SONNEY piece is produced in limited quantities across France, Portugal, and Italy, with long-standing manufacturing partners chosen for precision and care. Limited runs are not a marketing decision. They are the natural consequence of making things properly. When a piece is gone, it is gone, because the next one deserves the same attention.

i.

Material research

Every collection begins with material experimentation, not a brief. Lora works with the fabric until it tells her what it wants to become.

ii.

Print development

The trompe-l'oeil prints are developed in-house, from Lora's material fusion technique. No two collections use the same process twice.

iii.

European manufacture

Produced in France, Portugal, or Italy with makers who have been chosen for their rigour and their relationship with the studio, not their speed.

iv.

Limited and final

Each piece is made in a small run. When the run is finished, it is finished. There is no restock, because what comes next will be different.

The Jura is still in everything.

Rain on petals. Moss on stone. The smell of wet earth after a storm. Lora Sonney left Pontarlier and went to Geneva, to Paris, to Marine Serre, to Celine, to Jakob Schlaepfer, to AZ Factory's couture atelier at the Fondation Cartier. She brought the garden with her. It is in the prints. It is in the perfume. It is in the way a SONNEY coat feels like something you found in a forest that happened to fit perfectly.
